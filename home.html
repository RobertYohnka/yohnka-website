I grew up in Kankakee, Illinois -a small town, 60 miles south of Chicago. I graduated with a Bachelor's degree in Psychology from Lewis University and a Masters of Business Administration from Seattle Pacific University. My career to date has been focused on Higher Education and specifically, Research Administration.

In 2006, I moved to Lusaka, Zambia to manage business and finance operations for Emory University's research centers in Africa. Until 2010, I lived in the capital of Zambia and managed satellite sites in Northern Zambia and in Kigali, Rwanda. I returned to the US and have worked at Emory, adminstering research proejcts across the School of Medicine.

Throughout my career, I have found innovative solutions to complex real-world business cases. See a short list of a few of my favorite projects here.
